Homayun Afrabandpey

Research Scientist

Nokia Technologies

I’m a research scientist at Nokia Technologies. Prior to that I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Multi-Source Probabilistic Inference group at University of Helsinki working with Professor Arto Klami. I did my PhD in the Probabilistic Machine Leanring group under supervision of Professor Samuel Kaski in 2019. After finishing my PhD and before starting the postdoctoral position, I had the opportunity to work with some amazing people at Sony AI in Tokyo, as a visiting researcher.

My main research interests include:

  • Neural network compression: reducing the size of deep neural network models while maitaining their predictive performance as much as possible to ease their usage in decentralized environment.
  • Interpretability: making machine learning models understandable for human decision makers.
  • Human-in-the-loop machine learning: combining human and machine intelligence to create more powerful machine learning models.
